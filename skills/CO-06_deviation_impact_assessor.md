# CO-06 — Deviation Impact Assessor

## Purpose

Assess the supply chain impact of a reported deviation — temperature excursion, documentation error, manufacturing issue, or any event that may affect the usability or availability of clinical supply.

---

## Owner

**Primary:** GxP Compliance Manager

---

## When This Skill Is Used

- When a deviation or issue is reported (future workflow — Deviation & Issue Response)

---

## Inputs

1. **Deviation report** — Description of the deviation event
2. **Stock position** — From SI-01 (to understand affected inventory)
3. **Study configuration** — Protocol context

---

## Steps

1. **Understand the deviation** — What happened, where, when, and what stock or processes are affected
2. **Assess scope** — How many batches, sites, countries, or patients are affected?
3. **Assess patient impact** — Could patients receive affected product? Are patients at risk of stock-out if affected stock is quarantined?
4. **Determine remediation needs** — Does stock need to be quarantined, recalled, replaced, or destroyed?
5. **Produce impact assessment** — Scope, severity, and recommended remediation actions

---

## Output

- Deviation description and scope
- Affected stock (batches, quantities, locations)
- Patient impact assessment
- Recommended remediation actions
- Severity classification (CRITICAL, MAJOR, MINOR)

---

## Notes

- Deviations with patient safety implications should be classified as CRITICAL and flagged for immediate human attention
- The impact assessment feeds into the Reporting Agent (RC-08) for the Deviation Report
